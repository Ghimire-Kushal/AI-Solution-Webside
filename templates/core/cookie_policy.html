Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2026. This policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use, and how you can control them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and activity, making your experience more personalised and efficient. Cookies cannot run programs or deliver viruses to your device.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration Essential?
Session Maintains your browsing session (e.g. CSRF protection for forms) Session (deleted on close) Yes
CSRF Token Security cookie that prevents cross-site request forgery attacks on our contact form Session Yes
Analytics Anonymised tracking of page visits and user interactions to help us improve the site Up to 24 months No
Google Maps Set when you interact with embedded maps on our Contact page Up to 6 months No
CDN / Fonts Bootstrap and icon libraries loaded from jsDelivr CDN may set performance cookies Varies No

3. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are required for the website to function correctly. They enable core features such as form submission security (CSRF protection) and session management. You cannot opt out of essential cookies without affecting how the website works.

4. Non-Essential Cookies

Non-essential cookies (analytics, third-party) are only placed with your consent. You can accept or decline these using the cookie banner shown when you first visit our website. You can also change your preferences at any time (see Section 5).

5. How to Control Cookies

You can control and delete cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. See your browser's help documentation:
    • Google Chrome
    • Mozilla Firefox
    • Apple Safari
    • Microsoft Edge
  • Opt-out tools: You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking via the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Contact us: Email student.helpline@sunderland.ac.uk to request that we delete your data.

Blocking certain cookies may affect how parts of our website function (e.g. maps, contact form).

6. Third-Party Cookies

When you interact with Google Maps embeds on our Contact page, Google may set their own cookies. We have no control over these cookies. Please refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy as our services or technology changes. The "last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent version. We recommend reviewing this page periodically.
